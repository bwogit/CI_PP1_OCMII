About

The Bio


I have the unshakable belief that the Caribbean is a market that international businesses will have to contend with. From Cuba to Puerto Rico and from the Bahamas to Trinidad & Tobago, a myriad of business opportunities await partners with vision.


Born and raised in Martinique (French outermost region). I possess the indigenous desire to participate in the transformation of the Caribbean from a tourism dependant zone to a major economic player.


For the past 20 years I have lived and worked in Ireland in a highly advanced technological field within the very competitive sector of Higher Education Institutes (HEI). OCMii (Opening the Caribbean Market to investment & innovation ) taps into the important “HEI - Irish Industry” synergy, which has been responsible for the creation of successful international companies and start-ups. This synergy has been the main contributor to the establishment of Ireland as a global leader of Innovation. OCMii aims to become the perfect Innovation gateway into the Caribbean.

The Vision


The Caribbean region in general suffers from the emigration of its talent, which is a hindrance to progress and a disaster when there is no indigenous replacement.Even when the desire to come back is strong, our emigrated elite is reluctant to return home, for lack of the comparable work and life conditions which they are accustomed to. Providing cutting-edge innovation and projects would allow the Caribbean a to be key international player through:



  • The retention of our talent by creating and maintaining high-value jobs
  • Developing and nurturing businesses, scientists and talented people.
  • Attracting back to our shores our elite currently based in America, Africa, Europe, and Asia.

It is critical for the Caribbean nations to be active participants in the fourth industrial revolution (new technologies). Innovation is paramount for maintaining competitiveness in a Global Market especially for small countries. All over the world, digitization and the digital economy has enabled businesses to increase productivity and spur innovation. In recognition of this fact, developed country governments are making digital economy policy a top priority, working to empower their businesses and citizens with the tools, education and resources necessary to reap the full benefits of the digital economy.

Bridging Europe and the Caribbean


OCMii combines my knowledge of the Irish Innovation landscape and my strong desire to export it to the Caribbean Markets. OCMii aims to be a trusted bridge and a channel for economic partnerships between the Caribbean market and the Irish Enterprises and Innovation network. OCMii also has strong ambitions to facilitate on going cooperation between the all the Caribbean Nations and the EU through Martinique and Guadeloupe which have a native visibility and access into EU top Industry, knowhow, Innovation and projects thanks to existing economic, political and financial, policies and agreements.

OCMii capitalises on synergies between Enterprise Ireland and HEIs in Ireland. OCMii joined the TEAM (Trade Enterprise and Marketing) Open Innovation Network in the Institute of Technology Carlow in July 2021 as part of its ongoing development as an innovation broker focused on the opportunities for international trade with the Caribbean region. TEAM Open Innovation Network is an Enterprise Ireland supported initiative to connect regional ambition with international engagement and enterprise development, through a variety of enterprise and entrepreneurship development initiatives.

The TEAM Open Innovation Network gives OCMII access to the Irish HEIs and Enterprise Development Infrastructure both of which have strong international focus and accessibility.

The Solution and Services


  • Fact-finding trade missions to assess the needs in skills, knowhow or industry in the Caribbean in different areas, including IT, technology, education, services, finance and agriculture.
  • Partnering HEIs between the countries and promoting a greater synergy between schools and businesses directly in the Caribbean via incubators, spin-offs, start-ups etc...
  • Provide training, education, knowledge transfer, through partnering with businesses and academic institutions in Ireland with collaborating institutions in the Caribbean and in Ireland.

Bilateral business partnerships between Ireland and the Caribbean. Providing employment and work experience opportunities both in Ireland and the Caribbean Giving local Caribbean enterprises a presence in Ireland, e.g. in collaboration with IDA Ireland Allowing Irish industries to gain a better understanding of the Caribbean and its markets Promoting local supply chain and working towards a more sustainable future.
